Insights about the data/visualization:

  • New COVID-19 cases consistently declined during spring and summer months (April through August), suggesting a recurring seasonal pattern of reduced transmission during warmer periods.
  • The rate of new cases rises most sharply around January of each year, with the January 2022 surge far exceeding any prior peak — and given the steep upward trajectory at the time of the visualization, an even higher peak appeared imminent.
  • Case counts follow a broadly sinusoidal pattern across all three years, cycling between seasonal peaks and troughs — though the amplitude of each wave varies considerably, with each successive winter surge growing larger than the last.
